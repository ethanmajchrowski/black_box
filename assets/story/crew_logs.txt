CREW LOG SUMMARY

Crew activity was routine during the early mission phase. Maintenance checks and system inspections were performed after arrival in the system, including troubleshooting a temporary communications issue which was resolved through a reboot. Crew morale was reported as good and initial scans of the system revealed no immediate hazards.

Minor engineering concerns were recorded during the first day of operations.

Later in the mission timeline, engineering staff began noting irregular behavior within the communications subsystem. Diagnostics were reported as running slower than expected, and repeated handshake attempts were observed on the communications bus without registering as external signals.

During this same period, a crew observation recorded that Dr. Kael from the research team had been attempting to route a signal through the communications test interface for calibration purposes. A separate automated log also recorded that the research team requested temporary access to the communications diagnostics bus.

Shortly before the communications system failure recorded in the performance logs, engineering staff noted that the communications subsystem appeared to be receiving unexplained pings from an unidentified source.
